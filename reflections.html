reflections about me watch list

As much as I wish I could say I use the internet for anything else, generally my internet usage is for entertainment and to shut the brain up. The internet has become really good at sucking you in and subsequently sucking your brains out and quite frankly I’ve had my brains ripped out by the content machine quite a few times. Even while writing this I’m fighting the urge to forfeit my brains just so that I can dodge my responsibilities some more. While I may sometimes be given something of worth, like videos on linguistics and history that develop my love for the world, the majority of what I feel forced to watch is slop. But it is designed this way. Short form content sites don’t need you to like the content or the habit, they just need you to keep doing it.

In practically all the screenshots I was doing school work or actively in class. Everything is online now which includes class. I know that last sentence dives so deep and digs up a lot about this world that no one realizes so try to keep your mind in one piece after I say such perplexing and surprising things like school being online.

I think the internet needs to collapse to be rebuilt. Everything has gone to shit quite frankly and one day everyone else will stop seeing endless pop ups and ads designed to take advantage of you as a necessary evil to keep the internet going. Every website or app is becoming buggier and buggier and less user friendly but nobody seems to care because we are all junkies. Every day the internet feels more and more like nuclear science, where instead of its discovery and creation starting a nuclear powered revolution where everything is green and efficient, we chose to make bombs. In this timeline there is a possible future where in between breathing in nuclear fallout and coal plant polluted air I can watch a 30 second clip of a world famous streamer that nobody knew about until yesterday interrupted by 2 minutes of ads for AI girlfriends and tax relief scams. In fact, we’re basically there sin nuclear fallout.

my feelings about the future of the internet

click here to touch grass!!

The contrast of life online and offline is still one we have to understand. I think too many people see too much of a distinction between the two in an ever increasingly online world. Many may think their online postings and ramblings and such are distinct from the person they present themselves as in the real world without connecting that they come from the same place. It is this distinction that gives people the false sense of comfort that they can have a life where they present as normal and a life online where they are raging incels microdosing terrorism. But that distinction could only be made in a severance type world where the conscious isn't working overtime trying to bounce between both worlds that both exist in the subconscious. Someone may consciously think one way online and another offline but the subconscious thinks both ways all the time and online behavior will always manifest itself offline eventually.

There is also somewhat of an expectation to uphold this separation. There is the fear of coming off as “terminally online” to friends, family, and co-workers that stops people from connecting through internet topics. Here's an anecdote. The county I grew up in has its own meme page that like many topic based meme pages has devolved into a space for the admins own life, opinions, and vanity. When visiting home, I saw them at the main flea market in north county and approached them. What followed was one of the most awkward interactions with someone who when online is rather unabashed, solely because the gap between online and offline life was bridged.
